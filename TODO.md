## World-building facts
- VNMs (Von Neumann machines) have proliferated in the galaxy (not the whole universe, this is crucial) in search for the answer to the ultimate question: "how can entropy be reversed?", alluding to The Last Question by Asimov.
- All VNMs have failed due to the resistance of sentient lifeforms.
- Player can choose to cooperate with encountered lifeforms, but this will end up in extraction limits / inherent inefficiencies
- Or, alternatively, completely obliterate the lifeforms.
- In the end, player needs to do BOTH successfully, in order for the best chance of success of everyone else in the area.
- Player is equipped with a no-questions-asked time-resetter to have a go at it multiple times.

## Gameplay loop 
- At every chapter, there will always be a [Resistance] mechanic
- You are NOT guaranteed to win every chapter using the first world seed.
    - Re: prestige upgrades, they may be too weak to fend off [Resistance] pressure, resulting in a forced prestige
        - Re: efficiency / yield, also may be too weak to accumulate required resources to catalyst in range
- Each chapter will end in a "catalyst"
    - In short, this will be an event where past, present, and all possible future VNMs in-range are activated with an identical copy of your consciousness.
    - The player concludes this is the only efficient way to spread to other systems, but at a new level of abstraction, new challenges await.
- Each chapter's time granularity will be larger than the previous, e.g. Chapter 1, 1 tick = 1 in-universe second; Chapter 2, 1 tick = 1 in-universe week. All game ticks are 1 real-world second long.
